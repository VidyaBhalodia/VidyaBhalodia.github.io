Insurance and Gender

Percentage wise, approximately the same numbers of males and females were covered by private insurance. More women than men were covered by Medicare and Medicaid/CHIP, while more men than women were covered by Worker's Compensation or were paying out of pocket (Self-Pay)

Race/Ethnicity and Gender

Across all races and ethnicities, women were more likely to use the ER than men were.

Chief Complaint and Gender

Here we identified the 10 most commmon complaints for which patients went to the emergency room. The most common complaints for men and women both were stomach/abdominal pain and cramping, and chest-pain. What is most striking is that 2x as many women as men came to the ER when abdominal pain.
